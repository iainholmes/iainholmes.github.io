Iain Holmes is a Master's student in Economics at North Carolina State University, where he concentrates in data analytics and environmental economics. His research sits at the intersection of quantitative methods and environmental policy — applying econometric modeling and statistical analysis to questions about how infrastructure and environmental systems shape economic behavior and housing markets.

His current thesis examines differences in property value outcomes resulting from infrastructure mitigation versus institutional litigation in PFAS-affected communities across North Carolina. Prior work includes a cross-sectional hedonic pricing study of North Carolina's water infrastructure, in which he identified a statistically significant 22.3-mile amenity zone surrounding dam infrastructure using an instrumental variable approach to correct for residential sorting bias.

Iain has built hands-on research experience through roles at Veritas Economic Consulting and Environmental Economics Services, LLC, where he has contributed to natural resource damages assessments, stated-preference survey design, and cost-benefit analysis for EPA compliance proceedings. He also brings professional experience in investor relations and financial analytics from prior roles in private equity and alternative investments. He is currently a Graduate Teaching Assistant in NC State's Department of Economics.

He is actively seeking research-oriented opportunities in economics, environmental policy, or data analysis.
