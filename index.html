About Me

I am a second-year graduate student at North Carolina State University, where I apply microeconometric frameworks to environmental challenges. My primary focus is on **non-market valuation** and **spatial econometrics**, specifically identifying how environmental externalities are capitalized into residential property markets.

As a Research Assistant for Veritas Economic Consulting and Environmental Economics Services, I support high-stakes litigation and regulatory proceedings regarding CWA compliance and Natural Resource Damages (NRD). My work involves building simulation models in Analytica and managing large-scale contingent valuation surveys.

Prior to my graduate studies in Economics, I developed a career in the financial services sector, specializing in **Investor Relations** and **Institutional Reporting**. I have managed portfolios exceeding $9B AUM, ensuring operational accuracy and complex stakeholder communication for firms like Standish Management and Obra Capital.

My professional foundation is built on an ability to synthesize complex data into executive-level narratives—a skill I first honed as a Legislative Research Intern in the **U.S. House of Representatives**, tracking infrastructure recovery following environmental catastrophes.
